# The Founding of the Hippu Dynasty

## Summary

The Royal Rabbitu Dynasty, once the greatest ruling family of the Jungle, gradually disappeared after generations devoted themselves almost entirely to intellectual pursuits.

With the Rabbitu royal bloodline effectively extinct, Grandfather Hippu rose to leadership and established the Hippu Dynasty, beginning a new era in Jungle history.

He chose Home Jungle as the capital of his kingdom and became the first Hippu ruler.

# Beginning

For countless years, the Royal Rabbitu Dynasty ruled the Jungle from Ancient Rabbitu Jungle.

Rabbitus became renowned for extraordinary intelligence and scholarship.

Over generations, the royal family devoted increasingly more time to intellectual pursuits than to governing the kingdom.

Eventually, the Royal Rabbitu bloodline disappeared.

The kingdom fragmented into independent Rabbitu chieftaincies.

---

# Key Events

### Fall of the Royal Rabbitu Dynasty

The centralized Rabbitu kingdom ceased to exist.

Most Rabbitu territories became independent.

---

### Rise of Grandfather Hippu

Grandfather Hippu assumed leadership of the Jungle.

He founded the Hippu Dynasty.

---

### New Capital

Grandfather Hippu moved the capital from Ancient Rabbitu Jungle to Home Jungle.

---

### Recognition

Although many Rabbitu chieftains refused Hippu rule, the two Rabbitu jungles nearest Home Jungle accepted Grandfather Hippu as their king after witnessing his principled and compassionate leadership.

---

# Ending

The Hippu Dynasty became the dominant ruling house of the Jungle.

Ancient Rabbitu Jungle remained culturally important but was no longer the political capital.

The Jungle entered the Hippu Era.

---

# Canonical Consequences

The following permanent changes resulted from this story:

- The Royal Rabbitu Dynasty ended.
- The Hippu Dynasty was founded.
- Home Jungle became the capital.
- Grandfather Hippu became king.
- Most Rabbitu jungles became independent.
- Two Rabbitu jungles accepted Grandfather Hippu's rule.
