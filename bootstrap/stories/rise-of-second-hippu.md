# The Rise of Second Hippu

## Summary

Second Hippu began adult life with no kingdom of his own.

Assigned to defend the Border Jungle, he repeatedly demonstrated exceptional bravery in battle and expanded the Hippu Kingdom through military victories.

In recognition of his courage and loyalty, Hippu King rewarded him by gifting conquered jungles one by one.

Over time, these gifts formed the Second Hippu Kingdom, consisting of sixteen jungles.

# Beginning

Second Hippu inherited no jungles.

Recognizing his courage and physical strength, Hippu King entrusted him with defending the frontier from Border Jungle.

---

# Key Events

### Appointment as Commander

Second Hippu became the military commander responsible for protecting the borders of the Hippu Kingdom.

---

### Military Campaigns

Over many years, Second Hippu led successful campaigns from Border Jungle.

These victories expanded Hippu King's realm.

---

### Royal Rewards

After each significant success, Hippu King rewarded Second Hippu by gifting him newly conquered jungles.

These gifts were permanent transfers of rulership rather than temporary military appointments.

---

### Formation of a Kingdom

As more jungles were gifted, Second Hippu's territories gradually became a stable kingdom.

Border Jungle became its capital.

---

### A New Era of Peace

As Hippu King matured and his ambitions shifted from expansion to peaceful governance, military campaigns became increasingly rare.

Second Hippu's role evolved from conquering territory to governing his own kingdom and protecting the frontier.

---

# Ending

Second Hippu became King of sixteen jungles while continuing to serve as Commander under Hippu King.

His kingdom remains one of the closest allies of the Hippu Kingdom.

---

# Canonical Consequences

The following permanent changes resulted from this story:

- Second Hippu became Commander of the Hippu Kingdom.
- Second Hippu became King of sixteen jungles.
- Border Jungle became the capital of the Second Hippu Kingdom.
- The Second Hippu Kingdom was established.
- Hippu King's expansionist era gradually came to an end.

# Notes

Although remembered as one of the greatest warriors of the Hippu Dynasty, Second Hippu's greatest achievement was not merely winning battles—it was earning enough trust that Hippu King permanently entrusted him with sovereign rule over sixteen jungles.
