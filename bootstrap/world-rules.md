# World Rules

This document defines the permanent rules that govern the Jungle universe.

These rules apply unless explicitly changed through a future canonical revision.

---

# Canon

Canon is the collection of facts accepted as true within the Jungle universe.

Bootstrap files are the initial source of truth.

Later stories may add new canon but should not contradict existing canon unless an explicit revision is made.

If contradictions occur, they are resolved according to the rules below.

---

# Resolving Contradictions

The ultimate authority on public canon is:

**Hippu King**

If multiple characters disagree about an event, Hippu King decides the official version unless the truth is intentionally left unknown.

Some truths may remain permanently hidden.

---

# Public Truth vs Secret Truth

Not every true fact is publicly known.

Information exists in three forms:

## Public Truth

Widely accepted facts known across the jungle.

Example:

- Hippu King rules 176 jungles.

---

## Personal Knowledge

Facts known only to specific characters.

Example:

- Personal memories
- Conversations
- Private experiences

---

## Secrets

Secrets are intentionally hidden.

Characters may choose whether or not to reveal them.

Example:

The true origin, purpose, powers and history of Jungle Temple are currently known only to Bandar.

---

# Memory

Characters remember important events.

Minor details may occasionally be forgotten.

Historical facts generally remain reliable unless a story establishes otherwise.

Bandar possesses the most complete historical memory in the world.

---

# Stories

Stories occur in chronological order.

Stories may introduce:

- new places
- new characters
- new relationships
- new objects
- new kingdoms

Stories should not invalidate previous canon without explicit revision.

Characters grow and change over time.

---

# Death

Characters can die.

Death is permanent unless a future canonical story explicitly establishes otherwise.

Dead characters remain part of history.

---

# Titles

A character may possess multiple titles simultaneously.

Examples include:

- King
- Minister
- Commander
- Student
- Caretaker
- Chieftain

Titles may be:

- granted
- inherited
- earned
- gifted
- removed
- surrendered

Titles are independent of species.

---

# Succession

A ruler may choose any successor.

Succession is not automatically determined by birth order.

A ruler may voluntarily abdicate.

A ruler may be overthrown.

---

# Ownership of Jungles

Jungles may change rulers through:

- inheritance
- conquest
- gifting
- political agreement
- succession

When a jungle is gifted, the recipient immediately becomes its lawful ruler.

There is no fixed number of jungles in the world.

---

# Government

Each ruler governs their own territories.

By default:

- Hippu King directly governs his 176 jungles.
- Second Hippu directly governs his 16 jungles.
- Rabbitu Minister directly governs his Rabbitu territories.

A ruler may appoint officials to govern territories on their behalf.

Appointments may be permanent or temporary.

---

# Economy

Taxes belong to the ruler of each jungle.

Tax collection has no fixed schedule.

The ruler decides:

- when taxes are collected
- how frequently they are collected

Taxes are primarily collected as Hippu Coins.

Real goods may also circulate through production, trade, gifting, or taxation.

Examples include:

- bananas
- carrots
- milk
- curd
- Hippu Food
- Human Food

---

# Objects

Objects may change ownership.

Objects may be:

- gifted
- traded
- bought
- sold
- stolen
- inherited
- consumed
- destroyed

Ownership changes do not alter historical ownership.

---

# Relationships

Relationships evolve naturally.

Characters may become:

- friends
- rivals
- family
- allies
- enemies
- teachers
- students

Relationships may strengthen or weaken over time.

---

# Preferences

Characters have personal preferences.

Preferences may change through stories.

Examples include:

- favorite foods
- favorite places
- favorite animals
- favorite hobbies
- preferred companions

Preferences are not permanent personality traits.

---

# Species

Every species possesses its own natural characteristics.

Examples:

## Hippus

- naturally brave
- naturally strong
- lifespan of approximately 120,000 years

## Rabbitus

- naturally intelligent
- possess Dantashtra
- lifespan of approximately 100,000 years

## Humans

- lifespan of approximately 1,000 years

## Bandar

- effectively infinite lifespan
- extraordinary long-distance travel ability

---

# Communication

All intelligent species naturally understand one common language.

Translation is generally unnecessary.

Hippus additionally possess telepathy, allowing long-distance communication.

---

# Time

The Jungle follows the same calendar as humans.

This includes:

- years
- months
- weekdays
- birthdays
- festivals

Time progresses normally.

---

# Unknowns

Unknown facts are not errors.

If canon has not yet established something:

- leave it unknown,
- add a TODO,
- or record it as an unresolved mystery.

Unknown information should never be invented without explicit canonical approval.
