# Customs and Traditions

This document records the known customs, traditions, and social practices of the Jungle.

Unlike laws, customs are cultural expectations and may vary between kingdoms and families.

---

# Royal Customs

## Choosing a Successor

A king may choose any worthy successor.

Birth order is respected but does not automatically determine succession.

The ruler's decision is considered final.

---

## Gifting Jungles

Kings may reward exceptional loyalty and service by gifting jungles.

Receiving a gifted jungle is considered one of the highest honors in the Jungle.

Examples include:

- Hippu King gifting sixteen jungles to Second Hippu.
- Hippu King gifting two Rabbitu jungles to Rabbitu Minister.

---

## Court Attendance

The royal court is open to:

- Kings
- Ministers
- Commanders
- Citizens with petitions
- Foreign rulers
- Visitors unless specifically restricted

Visitors are expected to behave respectfully while court is in session.

---

# Family Customs

## Elder Sibling Responsibility

Older siblings are expected to care for younger siblings.

Hippu King personally looks after Little Brother and considers this part of his daily responsibility.

---

## Learning Never Ends

Even rulers continue studying throughout their lives.

Examples:

- Hippu King regularly reads Ancient Hippu Wisdom Books.
- Rabbitu Minister regularly studies Ancient Rabbitu Wisdom Books.

Wisdom is considered a lifelong pursuit.

---

# Educational Customs

## Respect for Teachers

Teachers are respected regardless of a student's social position.

Even members of the royal family may be punished for neglecting their studies.

Example:

Little Brother occasionally receives punishment from Giraffe Sir.

---

# Diplomatic Customs

## Preference for Peace

Diplomacy is preferred whenever possible.

Rabbitu Minister's mission to reunify the Rabbitu Kingdom is based on negotiation rather than conquest.

---

## Respect for Ancient Bloodlines

Many Rabbitu communities remain deeply loyal to the Royal Rabbitu family.

Royal lineage continues to hold cultural significance even after the dynasty's decline.

---

# Friendship Customs

## Visiting Friends

Kings are not expected to spend every day governing.

Personal friendships are valued.

For example, Hippu King occasionally spends weekdays or weekends visiting Bhaiya Ji instead of attending court.

---

## Friendly Teasing

Playful teasing between close friends is common.

Example:

Second Hippu and Bhaiya Ji jokingly call one another:

> "Anera"

The expression is understood as affectionate banter rather than genuine insult.

---

# Storytelling Customs

Stories are shared across generations.

History is preserved through:

- Written wisdom books
- Personal memory
- Oral storytelling

Bandar plays a unique role in preserving and sharing these stories.

---

# Respect for Secrets

Not every truth must be revealed immediately.

Keeping a secret for the right reason is considered honorable.

Bandar embodies this tradition by choosing carefully when ancient knowledge should be shared.

---

# Daily Life

Most inhabitants follow a familiar daily rhythm:

- Wake in the morning
- Carry out work, study, or governance
- Spend time with family or friends
- Rest at night

Individual routines vary by character.

---

# Known Cultural Values

The Jungle generally values:

- Hard work
- Courage
- Wisdom
- Kindness
- Loyalty
- Learning
- Family
- Friendship
- Honest leadership

These values are reflected in the well-known saying:

> **"Hippu always Happy because Hippu always Hard-Working."**
