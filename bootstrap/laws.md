# Laws of the Jungle

These are the known constitutional principles governing the Jungle.

Additional local laws may exist within individual kingdoms.

---

# Law 1
## Authority of the King

Every jungle has exactly one recognized ruler at a time.

The ruler possesses ultimate governing authority within that jungle.

---

# Law 2
## Succession

A king may choose any successor.

Succession is not determined automatically by birth order.

---

# Law 3
## Abdication

A ruler may voluntarily surrender the throne.

The successor immediately becomes the lawful ruler.

---

# Law 4
## Overthrow

A ruler may lose the throne through overthrow.

Subsequent legitimacy depends on whether the new ruler is accepted.

---

# Law 5
## Royal Gifts

A king may permanently gift one or more jungles to another ruler.

Ownership transfers immediately.

---

# Law 6
## Temporary Administration

Administrative responsibility may be delegated without transferring ownership.

Example:

Rabbitu Minister appointing Little Brother as caretaker of two Rabbitu jungles.

---

# Law 7
## Taxation

Taxes belong to the ruler of the jungle.

The ruler determines:

- frequency
- amount
- collection method

---

# Law 8
## Ministers

Kings may appoint ministers.

Ministers exercise authority delegated by their king.

---

# Law 9
## Commanders

Kings may appoint commanders.

Commanders lead military affairs without automatically becoming rulers.

---

# Law 10
## Court

Citizens may present petitions before the royal court.

Foreign rulers may also attend unless restricted.

---

# Law 11
## Secrets

Possessing knowledge does not obligate its disclosure.

Characters may legitimately keep secrets.

---

# Law 12
## Prophecy

Prophecies are part of recorded history.

Their existence is canon.

Their fulfillment remains part of future history until realized.

---

# Law 13
## Death

Death is permanent unless a future canonical story explicitly establishes an exception.

---

# Law 14
## Stories

Stories may permanently change:

- rulers
- ownership
- relationships
- places
- organizations
- history

Such changes must be reflected throughout the canon.

---

# Law 15
## Contradictions

When two public accounts conflict,

Hippu King determines the accepted public version,

unless the truth is intentionally left unresolved.
